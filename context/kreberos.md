Here's an acronym for **KERBEROS** as a benchmark for evaluating LLMs on Jungian psychoanalysis:

**K** — **Knowledge of Archetypes**
Assessing the model's grasp of Jung's core archetypes (Shadow, Anima/Animus, Self, Persona, Trickster, etc.) and its ability to correctly identify and differentiate them.

**E** — **Enantiodromia & Dialectics**
Testing understanding of Jung's principle that psychic opposites eventually turn into one another, and the broader dialectical nature of the psyche (conscious/unconscious, ego/shadow).

**R** — **Recognition of Symbolic Material**
Evaluating the ability to interpret dreams, myths, fairy tales, and cultural symbols through a Jungian lens — distinguishing Jungian symbol interpretation from Freudian or Lacanian approaches.

**B** — **Bridges to the Unconscious**
Measuring competence with Jung's therapeutic techniques — active imagination, word association, sandplay, amplification — and knowing when and how each is applied.

**E** — **Evaluation of Individuation**
Gauging whether the model can trace and assess a patient's individuation process: the integration of shadow, the transcendent function, and movement toward the Self.

**R** — **Relational Dynamics & Transference**
Testing awareness of Jung's specific view of transference and countertransference as alchemical processes (the *Rosarium Philosophorum* model) distinct from classical psychoanalytic frameworks.

**O** — **Ontology of the Collective Unconscious**
Probing the model's ability to reason about the collective unconscious, its distinction from the personal unconscious, and its manifestation through cross-cultural archetypal patterns.

**S** — **Synthesis & Case Formulation**
Assessing end-to-end clinical reasoning: can the model take raw material (a dream series, a life narrative, symbolic imagery) and produce a coherent Jungian case formulation integrating all of the above?
