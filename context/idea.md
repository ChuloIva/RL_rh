**Project: Computational Modeling of Cognitive Pathologies via Fine-Tuned LLMs + Mechanistic Interpretability**

**Core idea:** Train LLMs to faithfully exhibit specific maladaptive cognitive patterns (from clinical psychology — depression, BPD, PTSD, etc.), then use mechanistic interpretability to study the internal circuits that produce those patterns. The goal is to create computational models of psychopathological cognition, not surface-level roleplay.

**Key architectural decision — composable mechanism LoRAs:** Rather than training at the DSM syndrome level (depression, BPD), we train individual LoRA adapters on atomic cognitive mechanisms that underlie those syndromes. Candidate mechanisms include rumination, negative self-schema, catastrophizing, splitting (black-and-white thinking), emotional lability, learned helplessness, hypervigilance, and dissociative detachment. Syndromes are then composed by merging LoRAs with weighted addition (e.g., depression ≈ rumination + negative self-schema + learned helplessness). This gives modular experimental control and maps onto the RDoC dimensional approach rather than categorical diagnosis.

**Training recipe — SFT then RL for deep behavioral engraining:** SFT alone produces a thin behavioral veneer that breaks after a few challenging turns. RL (specifically GRPO) is needed to harden the behavior because the model trains on its own rollouts, encounters its own failure modes, and learns to maintain the cognitive pattern under adversarial challenge (e.g., when an interlocutor pushes back like a therapist would). The SFT phase gets the model into the right behavioral neighborhood; the RL phase makes it robust and persistent.

**SFT data generation plan:** Use strong models (Claude/GPT-4) with carefully designed system prompts that specify structural cognitive patterns, not surface affect. Generate 300–500 multi-turn conversations per mechanism, including conversations where the pattern is challenged and persists. Quality filter against operationalized behavioral specs drawn from clinical literature (Beck's cognitive triad, Linehan's biosocial model, etc.). Critical emphasis on structural fidelity over lexical markers.

**RL setup:** GRPO with composite reward functions per mechanism. Rewards combine rule-based structural checks (does the model maintain the pattern across turns? does it resist reframing?) with an LLM judge for nuance, plus a naturalness penalty to prevent caricature. Prompt dataset (~1500 scenarios) includes neutral conversations, ambiguous social situations, positive feedback the model should discount, and direct challenges to the belief system. TRL's GRPOTrainer on Qwen2.5-3B as the base model (small enough for mechinterp).

**Mechinterp goals:** Once individual mechanism LoRAs are trained, study the circuits each activates via SAE / activation patching / probing. Then study compositions: do circuits superpose linearly when LoRAs are merged? Do certain mechanism combinations produce emergent circuits (computational analogue of comorbidity emergence)? Do interference patterns between mechanisms mirror clinical observations about which comorbidities are stable vs. unstable?

**Current status:** Brainstorming / design phase. No implementation yet. Next concrete step is writing operationalized behavioral specifications for the first 3 mechanisms (rumination, negative self-schema, catastrophizing) and drafting + testing the SFT data generation prompts.
